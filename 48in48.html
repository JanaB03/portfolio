About the Project

48 in 48 is an innovative program that brings together talented volunteers to create 48 websites for 48 non-profit organizations in just 48 hours. This intensive hackathon-style event demonstrates the power of collective effort and technical expertise in serving community organizations.

During this event, teams of designers, developers, and digital professionals collaborate to provide non-profits with modern, functional websites that help them better serve their communities and achieve their missions.

⌛ Time Started: 48 hours

About Our Client

Meet Judy

Our client is the visionary behind sustainable farmers markets in our community. Her mission is to create vibrant spaces where local vendors and consumers connect while promoting environmental consciousness.

Through her innovative approach, she has successfully launched multiple market locations, empowering over 100+ local vendors and serving thousands of community members.

Our client, who manages the local farmers market, came to us with specific goals to enhance their digital presence. Her main focus was on creating a platform that would facilitate donations, attract volunteers, bring in more vendors, showcase upcoming farmers markets schedules, and highlight their various community programs. These needs guided our design decisions and development priorities throughout the 48-hour project timeline.

Donations
Enable easy online donations to support market operations and community programs. Includes transparent tracking and tax receipt generation.
Volunteer Management
Streamlined volunteer sign-up system with scheduling, role assignments, and hour tracking for market events and programs.
Vendor Recruitment
Online application portal for new vendors, including documentation submission, category selection, and market preferences.
Market Schedules
Interactive calendar showing market dates, times, and locations. Features vendor listings and special event highlights.

Project Journey

Building a website in just 48 hours required careful planning, quick decision-making, and adaptability. Here's how our journey unfolded:

Day 1: Kickoff & Discovery (Hours 1-8)

Our journey began with an intensive client interview to understand Judy's vision for the farmers market website. We identified five key priorities: donations, volunteer management, vendor recruitment, market schedules, and program showcasing.

During this phase, I led our team through an information architecture workshop, creating the site map and establishing user flows while our developer set up the WordPress environment.

Day 1: Design & Planning (Hours 9-16)

Once we had a clear understanding of the project requirements, I developed wireframes for key pages while documenting our design system. We established the color palette inspired by earthy, sustainable tones that reflected the farmers market's values.

By the end of our first 16 hours, we had completed low-fidelity wireframes and received initial client feedback, allowing us to move forward with confidence.

Day 2: Challenge Phase (Hours 17-24)

We encountered our biggest challenge when a team member had to leave unexpectedly due to a family emergency. This left us short-handed with only 24 hours remaining to complete the project.

In this critical moment, I stepped up to take on additional responsibilities, revising our timeline and reallocating tasks. We prioritized must-have features while maintaining our commitment to quality.

Day 2: Design Implementation (Hours 25-36)

Despite being down a team member, we pushed forward with high-fidelity designs, focusing on the donation system, volunteer sign-up process, and vendor application form—the three most critical features for our client.

I worked closely with our developer to ensure the WordPress implementation matched our design vision, creating custom elements where necessary and adapting pre-built components to save time.

Day 2-3: Final Push (Hours 37-48)

The final stretch was intense as we refined the website's responsiveness, tested all functionality, and made last-minute adjustments based on client feedback. We completed content population, ensured all links worked properly, and conducted accessibility checks.

With just hours to spare, we delivered a complete, functioning website that met all of Judy's primary objectives, ready for the final presentation.

Personal Reflection

The 48in48 experience taught me valuable lessons about design under pressure, adaptability, and the impact of purpose-driven work.

Lessons Learned

This intensive 48-hour project pushed me to my limits as a designer and taught me several valuable lessons:

Prioritization is Everything

When faced with limited time and resources, I learned to quickly identify and focus on the features that would deliver the most value to the client and end-users. This skill has proven invaluable in my subsequent projects, allowing me to deliver high-impact solutions efficiently.

Adaptability Under Pressure

Losing a team member halfway through the project could have been catastrophic. Instead, it became an opportunity to demonstrate resilience and adaptability. I learned that with clear communication and quick reorganization, teams can overcome significant challenges.

The Power of Constraints

The strict 48-hour timeline forced us to be creative with our solutions and make decisive choices. Rather than limiting our design quality, these constraints actually pushed us to find elegant, streamlined solutions that might have been overcomplicated with more time.

Looking Forward

Participating in 48in48 reinforced my passion for using design to create meaningful impact. The experience of helping a community-focused organization like the farmers market strengthen their digital presence was incredibly rewarding.

I've since incorporated many of the rapid prototyping and decision-making techniques from this project into my regular workflow, allowing me to work more efficiently without sacrificing quality.
