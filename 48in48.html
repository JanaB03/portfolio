01 The Client

Meet Uptown & Boogie
Healthy Project

Judi Desire, Founder

Food, Community, Opportunity

Uptown & Boogie Healthy Project is a community health nonprofit rooted in Harlem and the Bronx. They run farmers markets, community supported agriculture shares, composting programs, textile recycling, and a kids wellness club — making fresh food and healthy living accessible in underserved neighborhoods.

When Judi reached out to 48in48 as a client, the organization had programs running but no digital home to match its mission. They needed a full website — designed, built, and launched — in a single weekend.

02 The Brief

48 hours. A complete digital presence.

Design a full website for a nonprofit from scratch — with no existing visual identity — and hand it off ready to launch. That meant making decisions fast: sitemap, content hierarchy, design system, lo-fi wireframes, and hi-fi screens, all in sequence over a single weekend.

03 Architecture

Structure first.

Before touching any visuals, we mapped the full site — every page, every navigation path. With programs as complex as CSA shares, vendor applications, and a kids club, the information architecture had to be clear enough that any first-time visitor could find what they needed without help.

Complete site map — home, programs, about, waste reduction, market, contact, and join our team

"A messy structure is invisible to the people who built it. We stress-tested every path: can a first-time visitor find the CSA program in under 3 clicks?"

04 Lo-Fi Wireframes

Lo-fi wireframes let us move fast. Every page was blocked out in grayscale — layout, hierarchy, content density — so we could get client sign-off on structure before committing to any visual direction.

06 Hi-Fi Designs

Full fidelity, every screen.

With the system in place, every wireframe came to life. Green for growth and health. Bold headers that earn attention. Real photography so Judi's community and story could speak for themselves.

Landing Page

The homepage sets the tone immediately — bold headline, strong green, the U&B mark. The donate CTA lives persistently so supporters never have to search for it. Above the fold: mission. Below: how to get involved.

About Us

Judi's story is the organization's story. We gave the About page real photography, video embeds of founder interviews, and a team section — making the humans behind U&B visible and real.

Programs

Each program got its own section with imagery, location, and a clear call to participate. Farmers Market, CSA shares, and Pop Kids Club each read distinctly while sharing the same visual DNA.

Donations

The donation page had one job: make giving feel meaningful. "Your Impact" shows donors exactly what their contribution does. Tiered giving, sponsor options, and a simple application flow — no friction between intention and action.

Join Our Team

Volunteer recruitment needed to feel inviting, not bureaucratic. Real photography from market events, two clear paths (Volunteer vs. Board Member), and staff testimonials — making the ask personal.

07 Live Site

From Figma to live — in 48 hours.

The development team built directly from our hi-fi designs. By hour 48, UB Healthy Project had a fully functioning website — donation flows, program pages, and a volunteer application running live for the Harlem and Bronx community.
