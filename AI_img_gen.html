Introduction
AI Generation Demo Video

Large Text and Graphics Models (LTGM) have revolutionized content creation, but users struggle to communicate their exact intentions through natural language. This communication gap leads to frustrating iterations and suboptimal outputs.

User Frustration

Users without domain-specific knowledge struggle to understand system mechanisms and formulate effective queries, leading to a disconnect between high-level user goals and specific, executable actions of the AI system.

THE CHALLENGE

How can we design interactions that help users effectively communicate their intent to AI systems?

THE HIGH-LEVEL GOALS THAT DEFINED THE DESIGN

  1. Bridge the gap between natural language and system understanding
  2. Make AI fine-tuning more intuitive and efficient for non-experts
  3. Provide clear feedback about how the system interprets user inputs
The solution

Natural Language Input System

A comprehensive interaction design that combines component selection, vocal input translation, and natural language feedback to create more intuitive and effective ways for users to fine-tune AI system outputs. Our approach focuses on making the complex simple while maintaining precision and control.

Component Selection

Allows users to select specific parts of generated content they want to keep or modify, making the fine-tuning process more intuitive. Users can click on elements they like and provide natural language feedback about what to change.

Vocal Input Translation

Research

Understanding the problem

Our research combined analysis of existing solutions like VoiceCuts [CHI '19], Stylette [CHI '22], and Grounded Abstraction Matching [CHI '23] with our own user studies and prototyping to understand how users interact with AI generation systems.

Literature Review

Analyzed existing HCI research on natural language interaction

User Studies

Conducted interviews and observation sessions with AI users

Competitive Analysis

Evaluated existing AI interfaces and interaction patterns

Prototyping

Built and tested various interaction concepts

Research process timeline showing our comprehensive approach to understanding user needs

Key patterns that emerged from our research:

Insight #1

Users benefit from natural language feedback that explains system interpretation

When users could see how the AI interpreted their requests, they were able to refine their language and achieve better results. This transparency reduced frustration and improved learning outcomes.

Insight #2

Component selection helps users specify intent during fine-tuning

Rather than describing what to change in abstract terms, users preferred to point to specific elements and then provide natural language modifications. This hybrid approach proved more effective than pure text input.

Insight #3

Vocal input can be effectively translated into specific, executable commands

Users found voice input more natural for expressing creative intent, but the system needed to translate these utterances into structured parameters. The key was providing clear feedback about this translation process.

From this, we concluded...

The gap between user intent and AI output can be bridged through better interaction design that combines multiple input modalities with transparent feedback mechanisms.

This led us to ask:

  • How might we make AI system interpretation transparent to users?
  • How might we combine visual selection with natural language input?
  • How might we help users build better mental models of AI capabilities?
  • How might we make fine-tuning feel more like natural conversation?

...leading us to the design principles that guided our solution.

Design Process

From insights to interface

Armed with clear user needs and research insights, we began translating findings into design solutions. The process focused on creating seamless interactions that make AI generation feel more intuitive and controllable.

Initial Sketches & Ideation

Early sketches exploring different approaches to natural language input and component selection

Key concepts explored:

Our initial sketches focused on four main areas: producing low-level keywords from high-level interactions, providing faster and more intuitive ways to generate/modify images, suggesting new considerations for exploring diverse aspects, and incorporating negative prompting for better image styles.

Refined Concepts

Refined sketches showing the evolution of our component selection and feedback systems

What we learned:

The iterative sketching process revealed that users needed three key capabilities: image component selection for keeping desired elements, natural language feedback for system understanding, and vocal feedback integration for immediate adjustments. These became the pillars of our final design.

Final Prototype Development

Interactive Figma prototype showing the complete user flow and interaction design

Combined approach featuring:

Our final prototype integrated component selection for precise control, vocal input translation for natural expression, and a comprehensive natural language feedback system that explains AI interpretation in real-time. This multi-modal approach addresses the core challenges we identified in our research.

System in Action

Each interaction was designed to directly address our core research questions, ensuring that every feature reduces barriers between user intent and AI output.

The following demonstrations show how our solution addresses the key challenges identified in our research.

1. Natural Language Processing

Addresses: System interpretation transparency

Real-time natural language processing showing how user input is interpreted and structured

Intent Classification

The system analyzes user input to identify the type of modification requested - creation, modification, or refinement.

Entity Extraction

Key elements like objects, styles, colors, and modifiers are identified and categorized for precise control.

Action Mapping

Natural language is translated into specific parameters that the AI generation system can execute.

Why this matters:

By making the interpretation process visible, users can understand exactly how their words translate into AI actions. This transparency builds trust and helps users refine their communication style for better results.

2. Component Selection System

Addresses: Visual selection with natural language
Component Selection Demo

Interactive component selection allowing users to point and modify specific elements

Visual Selection

Users can click on specific parts of generated images to select them for modification or preservation.

Contextual Feedback

Selected components trigger contextual prompts that guide users in providing specific natural language instructions.

Preview Changes

Before applying modifications, users can preview how their natural language instructions will affect the selected components.

Why this matters:

Combining visual selection with natural language creates a more intuitive interaction model. Users can be specific about what to change without needing to describe location or identify components through text alone.

3. Intelligent System Response

Addresses: Natural conversation with AI
System Response Demo

Conversational feedback system that explains changes and suggests improvements

Explanation Generation

The system explains what changes will be made and why, helping users understand the AI's reasoning process.

Suggestion Engine

Based on the current context, the system suggests additional modifications or alternative approaches users might consider.

Learning Integration

The system learns from user preferences and adjusts its interpretation and suggestions for more personalized interactions.

Why this matters:

By making AI responses more conversational and educational, users develop better mental models of the system's capabilities. This leads to more effective communication and better results over time.

Reflection

What we learned

The Power of Multimodal Interaction

This project taught me that the future of human-AI interaction lies not in perfecting any single input method, but in thoughtfully combining multiple modalities. Voice, visual selection, and natural language feedback each address different aspects of the communication challenge.

Most importantly, I learned that AI systems shouldn't just execute commands—they should teach users how to communicate more effectively through transparent feedback and explanation. This creates a positive learning loop that benefits both the user and the system.

Transparency Builds Trust

When users understand how AI interprets their input, they become more confident and effective in their communication.

Multimodal is More Natural

Combining visual selection with natural language mirrors how humans naturally communicate about visual concepts.

AI Should Teach, Not Just Execute

The best AI interfaces help users become better at using AI by providing educational feedback and suggestions.
