02 —

The designer who studies how people think.

I came to design through cognitive science, not the other way around.

At UCSD I spent a summer and fall in an XR lab under Dr. David Kirshhe studies how jazz musicians think, studying how people distribute cognition across tools, space, and interfaces. That work changed how I see everything. I don't just ask "is it usable?" — I ask why people behave the way they do at a particular moment, and how design can work with that behavior instead of assuming it away.

That's why I end up in civic tech and healthtech. The people those systems serve are often navigating stress, limited resources, or contexts that default design never accounted for. Getting it right there actually matters.
