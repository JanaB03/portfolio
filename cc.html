01 The Crisis

When policy creates
invisible harm.

In 2023, the City of San Diego established new regulations prohibiting encampments on public property. The goal was public health. The unintended consequence was something no one planned for: outreach workers began losing contact with their most vulnerable clients — the people they'd been supporting for months or years.

People who relied on these connections for medical care, shelter placement, and basic survival were suddenly unreachable. And the crisis, as one worker told us, always happened at the worst possible time.

"Every crisis happens on Friday at 6pm."

— Outreach Worker, Father Joe's Villages

ABC7 News coverage of the encampment sweeps near Petco Park

02 The Problem

A perfect storm of barriers.

Through our research, we discovered it wasn't just the sweeps. There was a compounding set of communication barriers that made an already vulnerable population even more isolated.

#1

Phone Loss

Individuals lose an average of 12 phones per year to theft or financial constraints — making consistent contact nearly impossible for outreach staff.

#2

Constant Displacement

Encampment sweeps force frequent moves, severing the support networks and making it difficult to reliably reach mobile health clinics or scheduled services.

#3

Resource Blindness

Many clients are unaware of what's available to them. The current system distributes information poorly — resources go unused because people simply don't know they exist.

06 The Product

Introducing CommuniCare.

A device-agnostic communication platform designed to maintain human connection despite displacement, device loss, or after-hours emergencies.

No Phone Required

Access from Anywhere

"I've had 12 phones stolen this year."

Secure access code login means no phone number needed. Clients can reach their account from any device — library computers, borrowed phones, shelter kiosks.

  • Works on any internet-connected device
  • No personal information required to log in
  • Large, accessible interface for challenging environments
24/7 Support

Always Someone to Help

"Every crisis happens on Friday at 6pm."

Private messaging with staff combined with a 24/7 AI chatbot ensures help is always available. Clients can delete sensitive messages immediately for privacy.

  • AI support when staff are unavailable
  • Immediate message deletion for privacy
  • Resource guidance anytime, on any device
Privacy First

Stay Connected, Stay Safe

"We can't find clients after the sweeps."

Flexible location sharing lets clients share a current location, a future meetup spot, or a general area. Track the mobile health clinic to ensure access to medical care.

  • Share current or future location
  • Manual entry option for full privacy control
  • Mobile clinic tracker for medical access
Smart Filtering

Find Help That Fits

"I don't know what's even available to me."

Smart filtering surfaces only relevant resources based on location, availability, and eligibility. No more wasted trips to services that can't be accessed.

  • Filter by eligibility criteria
  • Real-time availability status
  • Clear requirements listed upfront
07 Design Principles

Every decision rooted
in real constraints.

🛡️

Privacy First

Every interaction gives users control over their personal information with immediate deletion options. For vulnerable populations, privacy isn't a feature — it's trust.

📱

Device Agnostic

Access from any computer or phone — no personal device required. Works at libraries, shelters, and community centers where people can borrow a screen for a few minutes.

🕐

Crisis Ready

24/7 support pathways mean help is available when traditional services are closed — because displacement doesn't wait for business hours.

🤝

Human Connection

Technology facilitates but never replaces human relationships. Every feature is designed to strengthen real connections between clients and the people who care for them.
