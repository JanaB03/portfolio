Chapter 1

The Human Cost of Disconnection

When the City of San Diego established new regulations prohibiting encampments on public property, they aimed to address public health concerns. But there was an unintended consequence no one saw coming.

"Every crisis happens on Friday at 6pm"

— Outreach Worker, Father Joe's Villages

Outreach workers began losing contact with their most vulnerable clients. People who relied on these connections for medical care, shelter placement, and basic survival were suddenly unreachable.

ABC7 News coverage of the encampment sweeps near Petco Park

Chapter 2

Understanding the Real Problem

It wasn't just about the encampment bans. Through our research, we discovered a perfect storm of communication barriers that made an already vulnerable population even more isolated.

The Phone Problem

Individuals experiencing homelessness often lose access to their phones and phone numbers, making it difficult to receive urgent alerts—such as weather updates or encampment bans.

  • Average person loses 12 phones per year
  • Can't afford consistent phone service
  • Phone numbers change constantly
  • Miss critical appointments with staff

Pattern Recognition

Synthesized findings into actionable design opportunities

#1

Communication Barriers

Clients lose phone numbers constantly due to theft or financial constraints. This makes it nearly impossible for staff to maintain consistent contact.

#2

Constant Displacement

Encampment sweeps and seasonal changes force frequent moves, often losing connection with support networks and missing critical services.

#3

Resource Awareness

Many clients are unaware of available resources. The current system makes it difficult to efficiently distribute information about services.

Video from our tour of Father Joe's Villages

Chapter 4

Introducing Communicare

A device-agnostic communication platform designed to maintain human connection despite displacement, device loss, or after-hours emergencies.

No Phone Required

Access from Anywhere

Problem: "I've had 12 phones stolen this year"

Secure access code login means no phone number needed. Clients can access their account from any device—library computers, borrowed phones, or shelter kiosks.

  • Works on any device with internet
  • No personal information required
  • Large, accessible interface design
24/7 Support

Always Someone to Help

Problem: "Every crisis happens on Friday at 6pm"

Private messaging with staff combined with 24/7 AI chatbot ensures help is always available. Clients can delete sensitive messages immediately for privacy.

  • AI support when staff unavailable
  • Delete messages for privacy
  • Resource help anytime
Privacy First

Stay Connected, Stay Safe

Problem: "Can't find clients after sweeps"

Flexible location sharing lets clients share current location, future meetup spots, or general areas. Track mobile health clinic for medical care access.

  • Share current or future locations
  • Manual entry for privacy
  • Mobile clinic tracker
Smart Filtering

Find Help That Fits

Problem: "Don't know what's available"

Smart filtering shows only relevant resources based on location, availability, and eligibility. No more wasted trips to services you can't access.

  • Filter by eligibility
  • Real-time availability
  • Clear requirements listed

Chapter 5

Design Principles That Guide Every Decision

Every design choice was rooted in the real needs and constraints of the people we were designing for.

Privacy First

Every interaction gives users control over their personal information with immediate deletion options

Device Agnostic

Access from any computer or phone—no personal device required, works at libraries and shelters

Crisis Ready

24/7 support pathways ensure help is available when traditional services are closed

Human Connection

Technology facilitates but never replaces human relationships— every feature strengthens real connections
